The Phones-R-Us project required the development of two precision casings: the bottom casing was modelled directly from a detailed client drawing, while the top casing required reverse engineering from a physical sample. Both components were designed in Autodesk Fusion 360. The final designs were exported as STL files, ready for the additive manufacturing phase.

The top casing drawing was provided by Phones-R-Us as a reference for reverse engineering. However, the drawing presented significant challenges that required careful analysis and problem-solving.

Final Dimensions: 114mm (height) × 60mm (width) × 11.7mm (depth)
Drawing Challenges: The top casing drawing had multiple missing dimensions and confusing cross-sections that made interpretation difficult. A key issue was that it was often unclear where exactly on the top casing each measurement applied, as the drawing lacked clear reference points and datum indicators. Some dimensions were completely absent, while others were placed ambiguously on the drawing sheet. To resolve this, missing values were calculated by cross-referencing other known measurements on the drawing and using geometric relationships. Multiple views were compared to determine the correct locations for each dimension, and logical assumptions had to be made where information was incomplete.

The bottom casing was created directly from the client's engineering drawing. Unlike the top casing, this drawing was clear and complete.

Final Dimensions: 114mm (height) × 60mm (width) × 13mm (depth)
Drawing Status: The bottom drawing was clear and well-structured with no major issues. All dimensions were properly labelled, cross-referenced correctly between views, and had clear datum references. This allowed for straightforward and accurate translation into CAD with minimal interpretation required.
