The Phones-R-Us project required the development of two precision casings: the bottom casing was modelled directly from a detailed client drawing, while the top casing required reverse engineering from a physical sample. Both components were designed in Autodesk Fusion 360. The final designs were exported as STL files, ready for the additive manufacturing phase.

2D CAD Drawings

Top Casing

Reverse Engineered Drawing

The top casing was reverse engineered from a physical part provided by Phones-R-Us. The drawing provided had several challenges that needed to be worked through.

Final Dimensions: 114mm (height) × 60mm (width) × 11.7mm (depth)
Drawing Challenge: The top casing drawing had missing dimensions and confusing cross-sections. It was often unclear where exactly on the top casing each measurement applied. The solution was to calculate the missing values from other measurements available on the drawing, cross-referencing multiple views to determine the correct locations.
Fusion 360 Drawing

Bottom Casing

Client Drawing Translation

The bottom casing was created directly from the client's engineering drawing. Unlike the top casing, this drawing was clear and complete, allowing for straightforward translation into CAD.

Final Dimensions: 114mm (height) × 60mm (width) × 13mm (depth)
✓ Drawing Status: The bottom drawing was clear with no major issues. All dimensions were properly labelled and cross-referenced correctly between views.
Fusion 360 Drawing

Fusion 360 3D Models

Top Casing

3D Solid Model

The 3D model was built in Fusion 360 using the reverse engineered dimensions. The top casing has a non-flat geometry, with depth varying across the part. Wall thickness parameters were defined during the 3D printing stage rather than in the CAD model.

Fusion 360 | STL Exported

Bottom Casing

3D Solid Model

The bottom casing 3D model was developed directly from the client drawing. With a consistent depth of 13mm and clear dimensions throughout, the modelling process was straightforward. The model was verified against the drawing at each stage.

Fusion 360 | STL Exported
