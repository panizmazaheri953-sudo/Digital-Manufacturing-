📋 What is PFMEA?

Process Failure Mode and Effects Analysis (PFMEA) is a systematic method for identifying potential failures in a manufacturing process and prioritising actions to prevent them. For the Phones-R-Us project, PFMEA was used to analyse the 3D printing process and FESTO CP Lab assembly line.

The main goals of FMEA are:

  • Identify critical components to ensure quality of production
  • Identify possible failures early to ensure production functions
  • Estimate risks before they occur
  • Reduce or remove cost of changes after mass production starts
  • Support continuous improvement

The Risk Priority Number (RPN) is calculated as: RPN = Severity × Occurrence × Detection

This PFMEA is based on actual manufacturing data from the Phones-R-Us project, comparing two different 3D printing platforms: Ultimaker S3/S5 Extended (initial trials) and Bambu Lab P1S (final production).

📊 Actual Trial Results vs PFMEA Predictions

Ultimaker S3/S5 Extended (15 prints):

  • 10 failed prints (66% failure rate)
  • 7 prints looked acceptable visually but 0 passed FESTO measurement station
  • Primary failure: Depth measurement reading 1.18mm (spec required 1.6-2.2mm)

Bambu Lab P1S (5 prints):

  • 4 successful prints (80% success rate)
  • 3 prints "perfect" - passed all FESTO stations
  • 1 failure due to USB interruption (not printer hardware)

Key Insight: The CAD design was correct. The issue was printer capability. Switching from Ultimaker to Bambu P1S resolved the dimensional accuracy problem.

📝 Student Reflection & Innovation on PFMEA Application

Through this PFMEA analysis for the Phones-R-Us project, several key insights were gained that extend beyond standard textbook applications.

Printer Selection as a Critical Control Point: Traditional FMEA assumes process capability is fixed, but this project demonstrated that printer selection is a controllable variable. The Ultimaker consistently failed the FESTO laser measurement (reading 1.18mm vs 1.6-2.2mm spec), while the Bambu P1S passed. This suggests that PFMEA should include "printer model selection" as a preventive action rather than treating dimensional variation as inevitable.

Integration of Digital Thread Data: An innovative modification applied was the integration of real-time monitoring into the detection rating. By using the FESTO assembly line's laser measurement as an automatic 100% inspection point, the detection rating for dimensional failures could be reduced. For the Bambu prints, the FESTO station effectively became a real-time quality gate, reducing RPN from 288 to 144 despite the same severity and occurrence.

Economic Analysis of 24/7 Operation: The capacity analysis revealed that 10 printers running 8-hour shifts equals the output of 4 printers running 24/7. However, the risk of unattended operation (RPN=144 for overnight failure) required additional controls: remote monitoring cameras, cloud alerts, and fire suppression. This trade-off between capital cost and risk acceptance is rarely quantified in standard FMEA but is essential for real manufacturing decisions.

Support Material Innovation: PLA support removal was identified as a failure mode (RPN=144 with Ultimaker). The Bambu P1S solved this through better interface layer design, effectively reducing the detection difficulty without post-processing changes. This demonstrates that FMEA should consider printer software/slicer capabilities as a detection control, not just hardware.

Conclusion for Phones-R-Us: The PFMEA process correctly predicted that dimensional accuracy (RPN=288) was the highest risk. Switching to Bambu P1S reduced the occurrence from 9 (66% failure) to 2 (20% failure), lowering RPN to 40. For 50,000 units/year, 4-5 Bambu printers running 24/7 with remote monitoring is the recommended configuration, achieving 80% success rate and passing all FESTO assembly stations.
