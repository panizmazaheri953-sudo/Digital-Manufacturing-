What is PFMEA?

Process Failure Mode and Effects Analysis (PFMEA) is a systematic method for identifying potential failures in a manufacturing process and prioritising actions to prevent them. For the Phones-R-Us project, PFMEA was used to analyse the assembly line and 3D printing processes.

The main goals of FMEA are:

  • Identify critical components to ensure quality of production
  • Identify possible failures early to ensure production functions
  • Estimate risks before they occur
  • Reduce or remove cost of changes after mass production starts
  • Support continuous improvement

The Risk Priority Number (RPN) is calculated as: RPN = Severity × Occurrence × Detection

A higher RPN indicates a higher priority for corrective action.

📝 Student Reflection & Innovation

Through this PFMEA analysis for the Phones-R-Us project, several key insights were gained. The highest RPN (400) was assigned to the operator injury risk from sharp casing edges, demonstrating that safety risks must take priority regardless of production targets. Traditional FMEA approaches focus primarily on product quality, but this analysis expanded to include operator safety as a critical failure mode.

An innovative modification applied was the integration of real-time monitoring into the detection rating. By proposing in-line vision systems for edge quality inspection, the detection rating for sharp edges could be reduced from 8 to 3, lowering the RPN from 400 to 150. This aligns with Industry 4.0 principles of smart manufacturing and real-time quality control.

Additionally, the FESTO assembly line's pressing station (Station 4) was identified as a bottleneck with RPN 240. Rather than simply increasing inspection frequency, a preventive approach was adopted: statistical process control (SPC) on incoming casing dimensions, shifting from detection to prevention. This reflects a continuous improvement mindset central to modern quality management.
