📋 What is PFMEA?

Process Failure Mode and Effects Analysis (PFMEA) is a systematic method for identifying potential failures in a manufacturing process and prioritising actions to prevent them. For the Phones-R-Us project, PFMEA was used to analyse the 3D printing process and FESTO CP Lab assembly line.

The main goals of FMEA are:

  • Identify critical components to ensure quality of production
  • Identify possible failures early to ensure production functions
  • Estimate risks before they occur
  • Reduce or remove cost of changes after mass production starts
  • Support continuous improvement

The Risk Priority Number (RPN) is calculated as: RPN = Severity × Occurrence × Detection

This PFMEA is based on actual manufacturing data from the Phones-R-Us project, comparing two different 3D printing platforms: Ultimaker S3/S5 Extended (initial trials) and Bambu Lab P1S (final production).

🏭 Production Capacity Analysis: 50,000 Phones/Year Target

Target: 50,000 complete phones per year (50,000 top casings + 50,000 bottom casings)

Print time (Bambu P1S): 2.5 hours for BOTH casings together (1 complete phone set)

Actual success rate: 80% (1 in 5 fails, based on 4 of 5 successful prints)

Working days: 260 days/year (Monday-Friday, 52 weeks)

8-hour shift (9-5, single shift) 600 ÷ 8 hours 75 printers 50,000 phones
16-hour shift (two shifts) 600 ÷ 16 hours 38 printers 50,000 phones
24/7 operation (recommended) 600 ÷ 24 hours 25 printers 50,000 phones
24/7 + 20% buffer for maintenance 25 × 1.2 30 printers 60,000 phones (buffer)

Recommendation: 25 Bambu P1S printers running 24/7 to meet 50,000 phones/year target with 80% success rate. Overnight operation requires remote monitoring (camera + cloud alerts), fire suppression, and automated job restart capability. For maintenance buffer, 30 printers would provide redundancy.

Economic Insight: Running 25 printers 24/7 requires significant capital investment (approx. £25,000-30,000 for printers alone) but achieves the target. Alternatively, 75 printers on single shift requires less automation but more floor space and initial cost. The 24/7 approach leverages the Bambu's reliability (80% success) and unattended operation capability.

📊 Actual Trial Results vs PFMEA Predictions

Ultimaker S3/S5 Extended (15 prints):

  • 10 failed prints (66% failure rate)
  • 7 prints looked acceptable visually but 0 passed FESTO measurement station
  • Primary failure: Depth measurement reading 1.18mm (spec required 1.6-2.2mm)

Bambu Lab P1S (5 prints):

  • 4 successful prints (80% success rate)
  • 3 prints "perfect" - passed all FESTO stations
  • 1 failure due to USB interruption (not printer hardware)

Key Insight: The CAD design was correct. The issue was printer capability. Switching from Ultimaker to Bambu P1S resolved the dimensional accuracy problem.
