What is PFMEA?

Process Failure Mode and Effects Analysis (PFMEA) is a systematic method for identifying potential failures in a manufacturing process and prioritising actions to prevent them. For the Phones-R-Us project, PFMEA was used to analyse the 3D printing process and FESTO CP Lab assembly line.

The main goals of FMEA are:

  • Identify critical components to ensure quality of production
  • Identify possible failures early to ensure production functions
  • Estimate risks before they occur
  • Reduce or remove cost of changes after mass production starts
  • Support continuous improvement

The Risk Priority Number (RPN) is calculated as: RPN = Severity × Occurrence × Detection

This PFMEA is based on actual manufacturing data from the Phones-R-Us project, comparing two different 3D printing platforms: Ultimaker S3/S5 Extended (initial trials) and Bambu Lab P1S (final production).

Production Capacity Analysis: 50,000 Phones/Year Target

Target: 50,000 complete phones per year (50,000 top casings + 50,000 bottom casings)

Print time (Bambu P1S): 2.5 hours for BOTH casings together (1 complete phone set)

Actual success rate: 80% (1 in 5 fails, based on 4 of 5 successful prints)

Working days: 260 days/year (Monday-Friday, 52 weeks)

Recommendation: 25 Bambu P1S printers running 24/7 to meet 50,000 phones/year target with 80% success rate.

Actual Trial Results vs PFMEA Predictions

Ultimaker S3/S5 Extended (15 prints): 10 failed prints (66% failure rate). 7 prints looked acceptable visually but 0 passed FESTO measurement station. Primary failure: Depth measurement reading 1.18mm (spec required 1.6-2.2mm).

Bambu Lab P1S (5 prints): 4 successful prints (80% success rate). 3 prints "perfect" - passed all FESTO stations. 1 failure due to USB interruption.

Key Insight: The CAD design was correct. Switching from Ultimaker to Bambu P1S reduced RPN from 288 to 64.

Student Reflection & Innovation

Printer Selection as a Critical Control Point: Traditional FMEA assumes process capability is fixed, but this project demonstrated that printer selection is a controllable variable. The Ultimaker consistently failed (RPN 288) while the Bambu P1S passed (RPN 64).

Economic Analysis of 24/7 Operation: 75 printers on 8-hour shifts equals 25 printers on 24/7 - a capital saving of £50,000+ but requiring remote monitoring systems (RPN 144 reduced to 90 with cameras).

Conclusion: Switching to Bambu P1S reduced occurrence from 9 to 2, lowering RPN from 288 to 64. For 50,000 units/year, 25 Bambu printers running 24/7 with remote monitoring is recommended.
